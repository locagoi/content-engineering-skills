# GEO Content Strategy (template)

> `/longtail` reads this to decide what to write. Fill it with your own goals and topics — keep it short and opinionated.

## Goal

_Get cited by AI answer engines and rank on the buyer questions in your space → qualified visitor → conversion. Everything else (impressions, rankings) is an intermediate metric._

## Who you write for

_Your ICP (role, segment, region). AI engines cite specific, data-rich content aimed at a clear audience — generic copy doesn't get surfaced._

## Target clusters

_The topic clusters you want to own. `/ai-visibility` gaps tell you which ones you're losing._

## What earns citations (the bar for every piece)

1. **A data signal as the reason to write it** — an `/ai-visibility` gap, a Search-Console gap, or original data you can publish.
2. **Specificity** — concrete numbers, named methods, clear claims. Vague = uncited.
3. **Structure** — answer the question in the first paragraph; Q&A headings + `FAQPage` schema; one `<h1>`, real sectioning.
4. **Provenance** — author + date, internal links to related pages, entity links out.

## The measurement loop

1. `/scan` your site → fix until ≥ 90 (see [GEO-PLAYBOOK.md](GEO-PLAYBOOK.md)).
2. `/ai-visibility` → find prompts where competitors are cited and you aren't.
3. `/longtail` → write the page that answers each gap, to the bar above.
4. Re-measure. Amplify clusters that start getting cited; drop the ones that don't.
